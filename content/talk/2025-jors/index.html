--- title: 'To aggregate or not to aggregate: Forecasting of finiteautocorrelated demand' draft: false date: "2025-07-25T13:00:00Z" url_slides: /slides/jors2025.html location: University of Leeds, Leeds, UK summary: "The paper deals with the problem of temporal aggregation in time series forecasting. The question is, is it better to use aggregate data? For example, is it better to use weekly figures rather than daily? Temporal aggregation is an intuitively appealing approach to deal with demand uncertainty. In this article, the authors examine the effectiveness of temporal aggregation for forecasting finite auto-correlated demand. They also conduct an empirical evaluation to validate the analytical results using monthly time series of the M4-competition dataset. The degree of autocorrelation, the forecast horizon and the length of the series are all found to be important determinants of forecast accuracy. The paper discusses the merits of each approach and highlights their implications for real-world practices." event: JORS Webinar date_end: "2025-07-25T14:00:00Z" all_day: false publishdate: "2025-07-24T13:00:00Z" categories: seminar tags: - seminar - CARBS lastmod: "2025-07-24T13:00:00Z" featured: false image: caption: '' focal_point: center preview_only: false projects: [] ---
